What question today has no answer? Artificial Intelligence (AI) can answer everything! AI has now become a "smart friend" close to everyone, including programmers. People whose job is to create digital systems that have become a necessity in today's world.

The role of AI in technology development is growing day by day. The role of AI is so significant that many people think AI can replace the human role in technology development. Its ability to solve technical problems quickly facilitates the coding process for programmers.

But this convenience actually brings a risk. AI's answers may appear correct, but in some cases, AI answers seem to hallucinate and are not updated with the actual situation.

Nevertheless, AI remains an effective and efficient tool for problem-solving in the coding process, but reading documentation is a crucial foundation for deep understanding and ensuring accuracy in problem-solving.

Trying to solve error problems in development only by asking AI risks being inaccurate and wasting a lot of time. The intention is to be efficient, but it actually becomes a boomerang for programmers.

## Documentation Guarantees Information Accuracy

We've all certainly bought goods that come with a manual book containing information such as how to assemble, use, and maintain the item, provided directly by the factory that made it.

In the coding process that definitely uses libraries or frameworks or even the programming language itself, it is always accompanied by documentation. Documentation is like a "manual book."

This means the information in it is guaranteed to be correct because it is written directly by the creator or the team involved in the product.

Documentation should rightfully be the main reference for programmers in solving problems that occur and be a source of deep understanding.

## AI is Fast But Sometimes Inaccurate

Artificial Intelligence (AI) is formed by many methods, one of which is Machine Learning, where machines are continuously trained to solve problems independently without human assistance based on previously provided data.

Because of this, AI sometimes doesn't know about critical developments or changes listed in the documentation of a technology. The result is that AI answers seem to "hallucinate" because they don't follow the latest procedures and appear outdated.

Prioritizing reading documentation is like providing navigation, analyzing, and filtering during the problem-solving process. This becomes a fundamental ability needed by programmers.

For instance, when we're on a journey, documentation is the map and AI is a local person who can be asked about road conditions when something happens outside the map's information.

Relying on AI answers means dulling programmers' abilities because it presents ready-made information that sometimes ignores the latest procedures.

By prioritizing reading documentation first and only then seeking help from AI answers, it is hoped that the problem-solving process in coding can be faster and more accurate.

Reading documentation is an ethical and intellectual filter to verify AI answer results.
