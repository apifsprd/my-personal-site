The ASEAN Mitsubishi Electric Cup (AMEC) 2024 will soon kick off. The football competition between Southeast Asian countries, formerly known as the AFF Suzuki Cup, will begin on December 8, 2024. Indonesian National Team Head Coach Shin Tae Yong (Coach Shin) and the Indonesian National Team coaching staff have conducted a training camp in Bali, only calling up U-22 players and 7 abroad players to face the competition that often serves as a benchmark for football achievements in the Southeast Asian region. But what is the real reason behind this strategy?

## Regeneration and Talent Pool

Coach Shin wants to give young players experience on the "big stage" so they become accustomed to the atmosphere and sharpen their mentality as well as experience matches at the international level. "As we know, opponents use their senior teams, while we bring U-22 players. However, we will build cohesion and create something good," said Coach Shin as quoted from PSSI.

The regeneration reason can be said to be crucial because talented young players often sink (their careers decline) due to lack of opportunities. But in my opinion, the reason behind calling up U-22 players for AMEC 2024 could be an effort to increase the talent pool, which has been a problem all along.

PSSI through Chairman Erick Thohir has repeatedly complained in his interviews that the talent pool or database of football players in Indonesia has not met the desired standard. "Indonesia's talent pool is still not abundant. In Brazil, Argentina, and England, they keep producing. Until now, PSSI hasn't even reached collecting 150," said Erick Thohir as quoted from Antara.

The existing talent pool is still minimal, ultimately making it difficult to search for players for the National Team who can fit into the tactical scheme desired by coaches, both senior and junior. The minimal talent pool can also impact the tactical variations that coaches can play on the field.

## Choosing SEA Games Thailand Over AMEC 2024

PSSI and Coach Shin remain confident that they still set high targets for AMEC 2024 while continuing to prepare the team for the SEA Games Thailand in 2025. "AMEC is quite a big tournament and is part of the preparation for SEA Games. These young players have great potential that can be developed to help advance Indonesian football in the future," said Coach Shin from PSSI.

## Preparing Players for World Cup Qualifiers

In my opinion, calling up U-22 players can also be to prepare players for the 2026 World Cup qualification matches that will continue in March 2025. The call-up of 7 abroad players who played on FIFA Match Day (FMD) in November is an indication that Coach Shin also wants to build maturity in players who lack playing time at their clubs, such as Justin Hubner at Wolverhampton, Raphael Struick at Brisbane Roar, and Pratama Arhan at Suwon FC, who are still struggling to enter the main squad.

Nevertheless, it's still unclear whether all abroad players can play in AMEC 2024. PSSI through Indonesian National Team manager Sumardji said, "For Justin Hubner and Ivar Jenner, there's no certainty they can join the National Team," quoted from Kumparan. Only Marselino, Pratama Arhan, and Asnawi can be confirmed to strengthen the Garuda squad for AMEC 2024. "It looks like it's not easy indeed. Arhan, Asnawi, Marselino will most likely still be able to join, the remaining players are uncertain," quoted from Kumparan.

Specifically for Raphael Struick, there's different permission from his club Brisbane Roar. "For Rafael (Struick), I heard Brisbane won't release him yet (group stage), only for semifinals and finals will they release him," said Erick Thohir as quoted from CNN Indonesia.

## Physical Condition of Main Tactical Pillars

Fatigue and unfitness became problems for Coach Shin's Garuda squad in the FMD in November. The short time gap between players gathering and match day, as well as players arriving from Europe, became obstacles for training player fitness. Fortunately, the National Team managed to defeat Saudi Arabia in the second matchday, although physical constraints were still visible in players like Thom Haye and Raphael Struick who started in 2 matches in the November FMD.

The opportunity for physical conditioning over a longer period—9-day Training Center in Bali from November 27 to December 5, 2024, whereas in the November FMD the squad gathered 5 days before the match—as well as calling up relatively younger players could be concluded as a solution to the physical problems that exist in the Garuda squad.

Fitness is an important part of Shin Tae Yong's 3-4-3 scheme. Players are required to frequently cover spaces left by other players and perform quick pressing to recover possession in order to control the game.

## Strategy or Speculation?

Coach Shin's decision to rely on U-22 players for AMEC 2024 can be seen from two sides. On one side, this is a bold step that shows a long-term vision in building the National Team's foundation. On the other side, this strategy could backfire if the young players are unable to compete with senior players from other countries.

In the end, only time will answer whether this strategy will bring positive results or not. What is certain is that Coach Shin chose a risky path that requires courage to take. Once again, Garuda fans need to be patient to see long-term results. "Even this U-22 National Team is having some difficulty with player call-ups, so maybe I would say expectations shouldn't be too high," said Coach Shin as quoted from Detikcom.
