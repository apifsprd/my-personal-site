Ahead of the third round of 2026 World Cup qualifiers, the new National Team coaching staff led by Patrick Kluivert and PSSI officially announced the provisional player list to face the away match against Australia in Sydney on March 20 and against Bahrain at Gelora Bung Karno (GBK) on March 25. A total of 27 players were called up, including new names such as Septian Bagaskara from Dewa United who has been shining and playing consistently with Banten Warriors in Liga 1 with a tally of 7 goals and 1 assist in 26 matches, Ole Romenij, and 3 players who just took the oath of allegiance: Emil Audero, Joey Pelupessy, and Dean James, although these last 3 names still have to go through other administrative procedures after taking the oath, namely federation change in FIFA data.

Of the 27 names called up provisionally, there is not a single player from the Maung Bandung team that is currently dominating Liga 1 through matchday 27. The local club contributing the most players is held by Dewa United with 3 players: Egy MV (midfielder), Ricky Kambuaya (midfielder), and Septian Bagaskara (striker). This team, which will be based at Banten International Stadium (BIS) next season, is indeed in good form, sitting in 2nd place in Liga 1 with 49 points below Persib with 57 points.

The absence of Persib players in Patrick Kluivert's National Team provisional squad has attracted considerable attention, especially since Persib itself dominates Liga 1 with an 8-point difference over Dewa United. The team architected by Bojan Hodak has performed brilliantly in 27 matches, with Persib recording 16 wins, 9 draws, and only 2 defeats.

The absence of players from the Maung Bandung team raises the question: is Persib's dominance in Liga 1 not good enough for the National Team?

## The Key to Persib's Success Lies in Foreign Players

In the 2024-2025 season, Persib registered a total of 9 foreign players: Kevin Mendoza (Goalkeeper), Gustavo Franca (Center Back), Nick Kuipers (Center Back), Tyronne Del Pinno (Midfielder), Ciro Alves (Winger), Mailson da Silva (Striker), David da Silva (Striker), and Gervane Kastaneer (Striker). With Kastaneer joining during the mid-season transfer window and Mailson also released mid-season, Persib currently has 7 foreign players.

Foreign players in the Maung Bandung team have indeed proven to be a solid backbone. Take Kevin Mendoza, for example - since his arrival at the beginning of the 2023 season, he immediately became the main goalkeeper pillar, replacing Teja Paku Alam. The top scorer and most assists this season are also held by the majority of foreign players. In Persib's top scorer list through matchday 27, there are 3 names with the top 2 filled by foreign players: Tyronne (12 goals) followed by David da Silva (7 goals) then Beckam Putra (5 goals). In the top 3 assists, there's almost no difference - the top 2 are filled by Ciro Alves (11 assists), Tyronne (4 assists), and Beckam Putra (3 assists).

The new regulation on foreign players in Liga 1 has indeed become a double-edged sword that continues to be a polemic among domestic football fans, allowing Liga 1 clubs to have 8 foreign players with a total of 6 players allowed to play simultaneously. On one hand, this regulation makes Liga 1 games more attractive and entertaining and can help close the gap between national football and international standards, but it has a side that makes it difficult for local players to get playing time.

Practically only Beckam Putra stands out somewhat in the Persib squad. Besides his goal and assist tallies, Beckam Putra has also managed to become a reliable player in Maung Bandung's attacking line. Names like Mark Klok and Adam Alis have actually played consistently throughout Liga 1 this season. These 2 midfield pivots of Persib have become an important part of Bojan Hodak's team navigating the tight competition in Liga 1.

## Are Persib's Local Players of Lower Quality Compared to National Team Material?

National Team player selection is inseparable from 2 things: tactical suitability and coach preference. Yes, it's quite possible that the absence of Persib players is influenced by these 2 factors. Let's try to examine some local player names who play regularly at Persib and then compare them with the National Team material that has been called up. In the defensive area, there are 2 local wing backs: Edo Febriansyah and Henhen Herdiana. Edo himself was once part of the National Team in the Shin Tae-Yong era and on several occasions even earned caps with the National Team. But it should be underlined that Edo's position (left wing back) must compete with names like Calvin Verdonk who plays in the top Dutch league with NEC Nijmegen, and Kevin Diks who plays in the top Danish league with Copenhagen. In fact, Diks became team captain when his team hosted Chelsea in the UEFA Conference League (UECL). Just by mentioning their club origins and matches played, the quality difference becomes apparent.

Take Beckam Putra again, who plays more as a winger. We can compare him with Ragnar Oratmangoen playing in Belgium's top tier with the Indonesian-owned club FCV Dender, or with Marselino who now plays for the English second-tier club Oxford United, also owned by an Indonesian.

Actually, the opportunity for Persib players to enter the National Team exists in midfield. Marc Klok and Adam Alis deserve to be called up by Patrick Kluivert, but the national coaching staff seems to have other considerations that ultimately chose Egy MV and Kambuaya who play well at Dewa United. Again, this is about coach preference and tactical suitability. Egy MV and Kambuaya are coached by Jan Olde Riekerink, who is also from the Netherlands, possibly understanding Dutch-style tactics better than Adam Alis or Marc Klok.

## Persib's Opportunity to Chase Back-to-Back Championship Wide Open

The availability of Persib players during this international break is a blessing for the pride club of West Java. They can focus on chasing their 4th championship title in club history and establish themselves as an elite club on the national stage.

Persib itself struggled when key players suffered many injuries, such as Febri Hariyadi (ligament), Rachmat Irianto (Achilles tendon), Dedi Kusnandar (fibula), Rezaldi Hehanussa (cartilage), Dimas Drajad (ankle).

Bojan Hodak, as head coach of the team from Bandung City, was reluctant to comment on the absence of Persib player names on the provisional National Team list in the Patrick Kluivert era. "I think this question should be directed to the National Team coach. I can't do anything because it's not my job to select Indonesian National Team players," quoted from pikiran-rakyat.com.

Certainly, as Persib supporters who love the National Team, Persib's opportunity for a back-to-back championship is wide open, and hopefully this National Team selection will lead Indonesia to achieve its biggest dream: the World Cup.
