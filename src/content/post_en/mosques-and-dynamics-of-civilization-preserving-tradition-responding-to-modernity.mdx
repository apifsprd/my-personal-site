Let's leave the Feast issue aside. Let's discuss one of the civilizations in the world, but before that, let's first understand civilization from its meaning. The word civilization means, as I quote from jagokata.com, "matters concerning manners, speech etiquette, and the culture of a nation." I emphasize the phrase from that meaning: "the culture of a nation."
I will discuss a culture in a civilization that was born between two great civilizations of its time, namely the Islamic Caliphate. A brief history shows that the State of Medina was formed and located between two great nations: Rome and Persia. The State of Medina lived in the middle world, according to historian Imam Anshari in his book titled "The History of Islam from the Peak of Baghdad."

## The Vastness of Territory Raises Social Problems

The tiny civilization in the midst of two great giants grew over time, from just the size of Medina to become a challenger to its two great neighboring civilizations, Rome and Persia.
In its history, this new civilization eventually clashed with Rome, a civilization that had been established earlier with its renowned military history.

History records that after these two great armies faced each other, they only stared at each other for weeks. The Islamic side saw Rome as a great civilization with a track record of war experience in various areas of its vast territory, but on the other hand, Rome was wary of this army because they were a force that had never lost throughout their wars. This story was finally ended by the determination of Islam's great leader in warfare, Khalid Ibn Walid, who boldly attacked the Roman forces.

In short, the 2 great nations that had dominated the world for centuries were conquered by a civilization or nation that emerged from the barren, desert-filled land of Arabia.

The expanding Islamic civilization raised issues of governmental administration and social order. We can imagine that the Islamic territory stretched from the east (Iran today) to the western edge of Europe (Spain today) with the complexity of its problems ranging from economic, social, to political.
An increasingly vast territory means an increase in population and cultural diversity in its society, which raises many problems, primarily about collectivity or cooperation to achieve a goal.

## Mosque as the Axis of Islamic Civilization

Mosque. A place of worship that is actually not only functioning as a spiritual space for Muslims but more than that. Its existence is like the axis of community activities that creates an effective social space. The existence of the Mosque makes the surrounding community frequently meet face-to-face and greet each other. The community remembers and knows who often comes to the Mosque for congregational prayers. When someone is absent from congregational prayer, someone spontaneously asks, "Why isn't so-and-so praying in congregation?" The Mosque, which initially made small community groups greet each other, gradually creates social bonds that quickly become a tool to strengthen relationships, and not only that, the strength of this social grassroots makes it easier for the central government to monitor and manage.
